The fair value estimate for Prologis holds steady at US$141.9, with no shift in the central price target coming out of the latest model update. That muted change is broadly consistent with recent Street commentary, where optimism around industrial REIT fundamentals is balanced by questions about how much upside is already reflected in the current share price. As you read on, you will see how this steady target fits into the broader bullish and bearish narrative and what to watch as that story develops.
Analyst Price Targets don't always capture the full story. Head over to our Company Report to find new ways to value Prologis.
BofA has a Buy rating and recently lifted its Prologis price target to US$153 from US$147. The firm points to the stock’s performance since Q4 earnings and views the recent pullback as an entry point linked to its view on the next industrial cycle.
Citi resumed coverage with a Buy rating and a US$145 price target, signaling renewed conviction around Prologis within its industrial REIT coverage.
Barclays raised its target to US$139 from US$118 and kept an Overweight rating. The bank remains Neutral on REITs overall, which places Prologis among its preferred names in the group.
UBS lifted its target to US$144 from US$137 and maintained a Buy rating, aligning Prologis with its broader view that REITs can benefit from what it describes as a potential sector turnaround.
BMO Capital most recently increased its target to US$137 from US$123 and kept a Market Perform rating, citing expectations for stable occupancy and Cash SSNOI across industrial REITs.
Baird downgraded Prologis to Neutral from Outperform, even as it nudged the target to US$130 from US$128. The firm argues that current valuation already discounts improved fundamentals and that further multiple expansion could be difficult without a stronger near term rent growth picture.

Prologis and GIC formed a US$1.6b joint venture to develop and own build-to-suit logistics facilities across major U.S. markets, starting with about 4.1m square feet and capacity for additional projects within Prologis Strategic Capital.
The Board approved an annualized dividend of US$4.28 per share, a 6% increase, and declared a quarterly cash dividend of US$1.07 per share payable on March 31, 2026, to stockholders of record on March 17, 2026.
Guidance for 2026 indicates net earnings attributable to common stockholders per diluted share in a range of US$3.70 to US$4.00.
A Los Angeles jury awarded about US$2.9m in compensatory damages and US$6m in punitive damages related to hydrogen sulfide gas exposure from a 2021 fire at a Prologis warehouse in Carson, California, with Prologis and another landlord found 7% responsible and more than 13,000 additional plaintiffs still in the case.
