Burford Capital has seen its implied fair value estimate move from £12.53 to £6.66, a shift that effectively halves the previous price target anchor investors may have had in mind. This adjustment lines up with recent analyst commentary that combines enthusiasm for the litigation finance model with more caution around risks and how to value the current case book. As you read on, you will see how to track these changing assumptions and what to watch to stay on top of the evolving Burford story.
Stay updated as the Fair Value for Burford Capital shifts by adding it to your watchlist or portfolio. Alternatively, explore our Community to discover new perspectives on Burford Capital.
BofA initiated coverage with a Buy rating and a 1,062 GBp price target, highlighting Burford as a unique pure play in litigation finance whose results are described as less tied to broad market moves.
BofA also points to Burford generating a cash return on tangible equity above its cost of equity and trading below tangible book value, which the firm frames as a valuation gap.
Wedbush, in its February initiation at Outperform with a US$14 price target, described Burford as a unique special situation opportunity with potential upside linked to the YPF dispute.
Deutsche Bank has lowered its price target more than once, including a move to US$15 from US$16, which reflects a more cautious stance when sizing Burford’s case portfolio and associated risks.
Both Wedbush and B. Riley have more recently shifted to a less positive view, cutting ratings or price targets, which underlines concerns around valuation, case timing and how quickly litigation wins may convert into cash.

Burford Capital reported the completion of a share buyback program first announced on June 5, 2025, with a total of 29,227 shares repurchased for £0.38 million, described as 0.01% of the company.
The company stated that between October 1, 2025 and December 31, 2025, it did not repurchase any additional shares under this buyback, with 0 shares acquired for £0 million in that period.
Investors tracking capital allocation now have confirmation that this specific buyback program is complete, with no further purchases reported in the final quarter of 2025.
The fair value estimate has been adjusted from £12.53 to £6.66, roughly half the previous figure.
The revenue growth assumption remains about 27.03%, with only a negligible numerical change.
The net profit margin assumption has been reduced from 37.57% to 29.40% on each $1 of revenue.
The future P/E multiple has been lowered from 15.36x to 10.30x in the updated model.
The discount rate used in the model has moved from 7.11% to 7.14%.
