RELX’s fair value estimate has been revised slightly lower from £37.14 to £36.67, while another analyst has raised a separate price target to US$47.20, underscoring that views on upside are far from uniform. The mix of higher and lower targets, plus a Neutral initiation at 3,325 GBp, reflects a market still debating how much to pay for the stock as AI related risks remain hard to pin down. Read on to see what is driving these moves and how to keep track of the evolving RELX story.

BofA, through analyst David Amira, lifted its Relx price target to US$47.20 from US$46, signaling confidence in the company at current levels despite ongoing AI headlines.
BofA highlights Q4 earnings as resilient, with little or no discernible AI impact on results or guidance, which supports the view that the core business is holding up against recent concerns.
Morgan Stanley cut its Relx target by 390 GBp, pointing to a more cautious stance on what investors should be willing to pay for the shares.
Citi initiated coverage at Neutral with a 3,325 GBp target, flagging persistent uncertainty around AI and indicating a more balanced risk and reward profile rather than an outright positive call.

RELX completed the share buyback program announced on April 24, 2025, repurchasing 29,767,742 shares, or 1.63% of the company, for £1,300m in total.
Within that program, 15,900,000 shares were bought between July 1, 2025 and February 11, 2026 for £750m.
The company issued earnings guidance for 2026 that points to expectations for another year of strong underlying revenue growth.
RELX proposed a final 2025 dividend of 48.0 pence per ordinary share, taking the total 2025 dividend to 67.5 pence per share, with expected total funding of £873m and a planned payment date of June 18, 2026, subject to approval at the 2026 AGM.
The fair value estimate moves from £37.14 to £36.67, reflecting a small reduction in the central valuation output.
The revenue growth assumption is held at about 6.44%, with no material change.
The net profit margin remains around 22.99%, indicating stability in profitability assumptions.
The future P/E ratio is adjusted from 31.30x to 30.95x, representing a slight trimming of the multiple on future earnings.
The discount rate moves from 7.95% to 8.00%, indicating a modestly higher required return applied in the model.
