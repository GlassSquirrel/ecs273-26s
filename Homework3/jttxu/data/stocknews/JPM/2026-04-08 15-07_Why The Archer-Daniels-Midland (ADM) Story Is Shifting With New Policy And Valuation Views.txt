Archer-Daniels-Midland’s fair value estimate has been updated to about US$63.82 from roughly US$62.09, a modest shift that puts fresh attention on how analysts are recalibrating expectations. Several firms are pairing this with higher price targets, such as moves to US$68 from US$61 and JPMorgan’s US$4 increase, as they weigh policy support, commodity trends, and a mixed sector backdrop. Read on to see how to track these changing views and what to watch as the story continues to evolve.

Jefferies, Barclays, BMO Capital, JPMorgan, and BofA have all raised their ADM price targets, which points to a cluster of more constructive views around valuation versus prior expectations.
Jefferies highlights the EPA's final Renewable Fuel Standard volume mandates as positive for ADM, which supports the case that policy can be a tailwind for parts of the business.
Barclays lifting its target to US$68 from US$61 while keeping an Equal Weight rating shows room that the firm sees between its fundamental work and where the stock had previously been framed.
BofA keeps an Underperform rating even with a higher target of US$57, pointing to concerns around commodities oversupply, an inconsistent backdrop for specialties, and a mixed setup in agriculture.
BofA also flags that potential catalysts such as PMI improvement, rate cuts, and capacity rationalization in China are still too early to rely on, which tempers confidence in a faster improvement story.

ADM Cares is committing US$500,000 to the Farm Forward Initiative with American Farmland Trust to support hundreds of farmers across six U.S. states with technical assistance, succession planning, resources, peer networks, and grants aimed at farm resilience.
ADM has completed a share repurchase program first announced in November 2014, buying back 185,235,951 shares for a total of US$10,122.9m, with no shares repurchased in the most recent reported period.
The Board has declared a cash dividend of US$0.52 per share on common stock, up from US$0.51. This marks ADM’s 377th consecutive quarterly dividend and 53rd consecutive year of dividend growth, payable on March 10, 2026.
ADM and Alltech have launched Akralos Animal Nutrition, a North American animal nutrition joint venture combining Hubbard Feeds, Masterfeeds, and ADM’s U.S. feed operations. ADM has also settled an SEC matter related to intersegment sales reporting with a US$40m payment and related changes to financial leadership and controls.
Fair value is updated to about US$63.82 from roughly US$62.09, a change of around 2.8%.
Revenue growth is maintained at roughly 4.73%.
Net profit margin is kept effectively flat at about 2.29%.
Future P/E is nudged up from about 17.36x to roughly 17.84x.
Discount rate is held essentially steady at about 6.98%.
