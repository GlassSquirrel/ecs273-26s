UnitedHealth (NYSE:UNH) and other health insurers are getting a lift after a better than expected Medicare Advantage update changed the tone overnight.
The government now expects payments to rise 2.48% in 2027, which is a big jump from the 0.09% increase that was floated earlier this year. That difference matters more than it sounds. These rates directly impact how insurers price plans and what kind of margins they can earn. So when expectations shift, stocks tend to move quickly. Alignment Healthcare (NASDAQ:ALHC) jumped 12.5% after hours, Humana (NYSE:HUM) gained 11%, and UnitedHealth rose 7.8%. Elevance (NYSE:ELV) was up 6.7%, while Centene (NYSE:CNC) added 4.3%.

What is really happening here is a reset in expectations. Investors had been bracing for a much tighter environment, and this update gives the group a bit more breathing room. That said, not every name looks equally strong on the fundamentals side, with quant scores still mixed across the sector.
