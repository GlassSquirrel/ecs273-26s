Chevron has resumed natural gas production at the Leviathan offshore field in Israel after a government ordered suspension tied to regional security concerns.
The company has added new infrastructure, including a third pipeline and platform upgrades, to increase capacity at the field.
Gas from Leviathan supplies markets in Israel, Egypt, and Jordan and is linked to longer term plans for higher export volumes to Europe.
For investors tracking NYSE:CVX, Leviathan is one of Chevron's key international gas assets, and operational status at the field can be relevant to its broader production mix. The restart and capacity build out come as the share price stands at $201.54, with the stock up 29.3% year to date and 53.8% over the past year. Over a 5 year period, Chevron has returned 142.8%, which may shape how some investors frame this latest operational update.
The new infrastructure at Leviathan, including the additional pipeline and platform work, may enable Chevron to move more gas to regional buyers once flows stabilise. Investors watching NYSE:CVX may see future updates on export agreements, regulatory decisions, or further capacity projects as important context for how this asset relates to Chevron's role in regional energy supply.

Leviathan coming back online with higher capacity gives Chevron more optionality in its gas focused business model at a time when security concerns in the region remain elevated. The extra pipeline and platform work at a field that supplies Israel, Egypt, and Jordan can help Chevron support existing contracts and, over time, pursue export growth into Europe, alongside LNG volumes from assets such as Wheatstone and Gorgon. For you as a shareholder, this update sits alongside other recent operational and geopolitical developments that have been influencing NYSE:CVX, including outages in Australia, higher crude prices tied to Middle East tensions, and new gas to power projects connected to AI data centers. Leviathan’s restart does not change the broader dependence on hydrocarbons or the execution risks flagged by analysts, but it adds another producing asset that can support Chevron’s integrated position relative to peers such as Exxon Mobil and BP.
The Leviathan restart supports the narrative that Chevron’s large, low cost resource base can underpin long term revenue and cash generation, particularly where gas volumes feed into regions with structural energy demand.
