Anthropic is starting to frame the next phase of AI deployment less around speed and more around control, and that shift could matter for how investors think about the rollout curve of advanced models. The company has introduced Project Glasswing, bringing in partners including Amazon.com (NASDAQ:AMZN), Apple (NASDAQ:AAPL), Microsoft (NASDAQ:MSFT) and Cisco Systems (NASDAQ:CSCO) to access a more powerful, unreleased model called Mythos. Rather than pushing toward a near-term launch, Anthropic appears to be using a tightly controlled environment to surface vulnerabilities early, which could influence how guardrails are built before any broader release.

The underlying concern is becoming harder to ignore. More capable AI systems could also expand the toolkit available to cybercriminals and state-backed actors, particularly in identifying weaknesses in code and bypassing defenses. Anthropic is positioning Glasswing as a way to give defenders a head start, allowing participating companies to test Mythos, identify flaws, and share findings across the group. The company has also engaged with US officials and pointed to existing work with the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency and the National Institute of Standards and Technology, suggesting that coordination could extend beyond the private sector as these systems evolve.
What may stand out is that Mythos was not built specifically for cybersecurity, yet it has already uncovered issues that had gone undetected for years. According to Anthropic, the model identified a 27-year-old bug in critical internet software and a 16-year-old vulnerability in video game code that automated testing tools had scanned millions of times without detection. Access to the model is being tightly controlled within Glasswing, and feedback from the initiative is expected to shape future safeguards. For investors, the signal could be that frontier AI development is increasingly tied to risk management as much as raw capability, with early-stage partnerships potentially becoming a key part of how leading models are brought to market.
